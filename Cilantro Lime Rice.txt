Chipotle Mexican Cilantro Lime Basmati Rice
<h4>Ingredients</h4>
<ul>
	<li>1 teaspoon vegetable oil or butter</li>
	<li>2/3 cup white basmati rice</li>
	<li>Juice of 1 lime</li>
	<li>1 cup water</li>
	<li>1/2 teaspoon salt (KOSHER)</li>
	<li>2 teaspoons coarsely chopped fresh cilantro</li>
</ul>
<h4>Instructions</h4>
In a heavy 2-quart saucepan, heat the oil or butter over low heat. If using butter, stir it occasionally until melted. Add the rice and lime juice and stir for 1 minute. Add the water and salt. Raise the heat and bring to a boil. Cover, turn down the heat to low, and simmer until the rice is tender and the water is absorbed, about 25 minutes. Fluff the rice with a fork and stir in the cilantro.

<ul>
	<li>1 cup extra long grain rice or basmati rice</li>
	<li>1/2 lime, juice of</li>
	<li>2 cups water</li>
	<li>1 tsp salt</li>
	<li>3 tbsp fresh chopped cilantro</li>
	<li>3 tsp vegetable oil (says she used for 4 but changed to 3 for cals)</li>
</ul>
Directions:

In a small heavy pot, add rice, water, 1 tsp oil and salt. Boil on high until most of the water evaporates. When the water just skims the top of the rice, reduce to low and cover about 15 minutes. Shut off flame and keep covered an additional 5 minute.

In a medium bowl, combine chopped cilantro, lime juice, rice and remaining oil and toss until completely mixed.
